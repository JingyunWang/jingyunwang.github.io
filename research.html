这本论文，是我在乌得勒支大学完成的博士旅程，也是整座「青少年自主 × 关系 × 学校转衔」研究岛的核心。 它把自我决定理论、生态系统视角和密集纵向数据放在一起，看清孩子在现实生活中的起伏。

This thesis, completed at Utrecht University, sits at the centre of my work on autonomy, relationships, and school transitions. It brings SDT, an ecological lens, and intensive longitudinal data together to trace adolescents’ real-life ups and downs.

DESIGN & KEY INSIGHTS DESIGN & KEY INSIGHTS
  • 日记研究：在同一个孩子身上看到——今天多一点父母温暖，明天就多一点自主。
  • 半年追踪：自主水平升高，伴随着父母心理控制在之后逐渐降低。
  • 学校层面：更强的学校联结预测之后更高的内在学业动机； 学业压力与负向师生关系，则推高受控动机。
  • 结论：当家庭和学校同时支持自主与联结时，孩子会更有弹性与方向感地走过转衔。
  • Daily diary: For the same teen, more parental warmth today predicts more autonomy tomorrow.
  • Six-month follow-ups: higher autonomy is followed by lower perceived psychological control over time.
  • School: stronger school relatedness predicts later intrinsic motivation, whereas pressure and negative teacher ties feed more controlled motivation.
  • In short: when home and school support both autonomy and relatedness, adolescents navigate transitions with more resilience and direction.

在研究上，我会继续：

In research, I aim to:

  • 用更多密集纵向与日记设计，追踪孩子和成人在压力高峰期的细微波动。
  • 把学习动机、亲子和人际关系、自主性与社会支持放进同一张图里，看清系统如何联动。
  • 探索AI 与心理健康的交界：如何让技术成为陪伴与赋能，而非额外的控制或评判。
  • 在研究中加入自我探索和生命意义的维度，理解人如何在不确定里找到可活的方向。
  • Use more intensive longitudinal and diary designs to follow fine-grained changes during high-stress periods.
  • Model motivation, family & interpersonal ties, autonomy, and support together to see system-level dynamics.
  • Explore AI & mental health: how technology can accompany and empower, rather than control or judge.
  • Bring self-exploration and meaning in life into empirical work to understand how people orient themselves in uncertainty.

无论是科研合作，还是课程、工作坊、咨询与团体，我都会坚持： 每一个结论，都既经得起数据推敲，也经得起真实生活的考验。

In collaborations, teaching, workshops, and therapy, I hold to one standard: every insight should withstand both data and real life.
