THEME 01 · SCHOOL & MOTIVATION THEME 01 · SCHOOL & MOTIVATION
学校转衔中的学业动机与压力
Academic motivation & stress across school transitions

关注从小学到中学的关键转折期：
当成绩、老师风格、学校氛围都在同时变化时，孩子的学业动机如何起伏？ 哪些压力会吞掉好奇心，哪些连结又能把热情重新点燃？

I follow adolescents through the shift into secondary school: when grades, teachers, and climate all change, how does motivation rise or fall? Which stressors drain curiosity, and which connections reignite it?

  • 纵向追踪学业动机、学校联结与家庭/学校压力的轨迹。
  • 区分「同一个孩子今天的起伏」和「不同孩子之间的差异」。
  • 将结果转化为：学校可以做什么、家长可以怎么陪伴。
  • Track trajectories of motivation, school connectedness, and stress.
  • Separate within-person fluctuations from between-person differences.
  • Translate findings into concrete steps for schools and families.
THEME 02 · FAMILY & PEERS THEME 02 · FAMILY & PEERS
在亲子与同伴关系中长出的自主
Growing autonomy within parenting and friendships

一连串纵向研究，追问一个简单却不容易的问题：
「孩子到底是被塑造的产物，还是能回头塑造关系的主体？」

A series of longitudinal studies ask a deceptively simple question:
Are adolescents merely shaped by parents and peers, or can they reshape those relationships in return?

  • 探索父母自主支持/心理控制、同伴支持与青少年自主发展的双向关联。
  • 考察青少年如何在面对同伴压力时，保有自己的立场与选择。
  • 为「如何同时爱孩子、又放手」提供实证答案。
  • Study bidirectional links between parenting, peer support, and autonomy.
  • Examine how teens resist peer pressure and keep their own voice.
  • Offer evidence-based guidance on loving while letting go.
THEME 03 · COMPASSION & MINORITY STRESS THEME 03 · COMPASSION & MINORITY STRESS
害怕被爱的人，如何学会靠近温柔
Fears of compassion, minority stress & mental health

这些研究从恐惧慈悲、情绪进食、性/别少数群体的社会支持切入， 关注的是：当一个人长期被否定时，他/她如何也开始不敢靠近温柔？

These studies look at fears of compassion, emotional eating, and social support among LGBTQ+ people, asking how chronic invalidation teaches us to fear the very care we need.

  • 在中国文化脉络下，验证「害怕慈悲」量表并探讨其机制。
  • 理解性/别少数如何在有限家庭支持下，依靠同伴与社群活下去。
  • 把结果转化为：更安全的团体空间与咨询介入。
  • Validate fear of compassion scales in Chinese contexts.
  • Show how LGBTQ+ people rely on peers and community when family support is thin.
  • Inform safer group spaces and clinical interventions.

DESIGN & KEY INSIGHTS

· 日记研究：逐日追踪父母温暖与青少年自主，看到「今天多一点温暖，明天多一点自主」。
· 半年追踪：区分「短期波动」与「长期轨迹」，理解学校转衔如何重塑动机。
· 结果指向：在家庭与学校同时支持自主与联结时，孩子的动机与心理健康更稳、更有弹性。

· Daily diary work: day-to-day links between parental warmth and autonomy (“more warmth today, more autonomy tomorrow”).
· Six-month designs: separate short-term fluctuations from long-term trajectories across school transitions.
· Conclusion: when home and school both support autonomy and relatedness, motivation and well-being become more stable and resilient.

  • Re-read “very obedient” as a child who has learned to hide themselves too well.

博士论文 Thesis
Autonomously Connected：青少年在家庭、同伴与学校中的自主与联结
Autonomously Connected: Adolescent Autonomy in the Contexts of Family, Peer, and School
研究方向 Focus

以自我决定理论为框架，考察青少年在家庭、同伴与学校三大情境中， 自主与联结的双向关联，尤其是在学校转衔过程中的变化。

Using Self-Determination Theory, the thesis examines bidirectional links between autonomy and relatedness across family, peers, and school, especially during school transitions.

方法 & 主要结果 Methods & key findings

· 日记研究 + 半年追踪，区分个体内波动与个体间差异。
· 父母温暖与青少年自主之间存在当天与次日的双向影响。
· 自主水平升高，与半年后父母心理控制降低有关。
· 学校联结预测之后更高的内在学业动机；学业压力与不良师生关系，则与受控动机和动机下降相关。

· Daily diary + 6-month longitudinal designs distinguish within- from between-person effects.
· Bidirectional day-to-day links between parental warmth and autonomy.
· Increases in autonomy predicted later reductions in perceived psychological control.
· School connectedness predicted higher later intrinsic motivation, while academic pressure and negative teacher relationships were tied to more controlled motivation and declines.

Take-home message Take-home message

自主与联结不是对立面： 每一点真实的温暖与联结，都在为孩子的自主「打底」；每一点被认真对待的自主，也在促成更少控制、更平等的关系。

Autonomy and relatedness are not enemies: everyday warmth lays the ground for autonomy, and genuine autonomy gradually invites less control and more equal relationships.

学校转衔 · 学业动机 School transition · motivation
中学转衔中的学业动机与家庭/学校压力轨迹
The trajectories and associations between academic motivation and school-related stressors in family and school during the secondary transition
研究方向 Focus

从「时间」的角度，看孩子在小升初阶段， 学业动机如何与家庭和学校的压力共同变化。

Follows how academic motivation changes alongside family and school stress across the transition to secondary school.

方法 & 主要结果 Methods & key findings

· 多波次纵向追踪，建模动机与压力的轨迹。
· 找到不同的「动机—压力组合型」：有的孩子在压力升高时动机明显下降，有的则相对稳定。
· 家庭与学校中更持续的情绪压力，与较高的受控动机和更低的内在动机相关。

· Multi-wave longitudinal design models joint trajectories of motivation and stress.
· Identifies distinct “motivation–stress profiles”: some adolescents’ motivation drops as stress climbs, others remain more stable.
· Ongoing family and school stress is linked to more controlled and less intrinsic motivation.

Take-home message

中学转衔不只是课程难度变了，而是 一整个压力系统同时在变化。与其责怪孩子「不上进」，不如一起调整家庭期望与学校要求，让好奇心有空间存活。

The transition is not only about harder courses, but about a shifting stress system. Instead of blaming students for being “unmotivated”, we can adjust expectations at home and demands at school so curiosity can breathe.

The issue isn’t only “too much phone time”; it’s what kind of online world adolescents inhabit and whether they face attacks alone. Effective intervention changes climate and support, not just screen time.

不再只看「谁的成绩高」，而是追踪同一个孩子：

Rather than only comparing students, the study asks: for the same adolescent, when they feel more capable and connected than usual, does intrinsic motivation also rise?

· Multi-wave design with within-person analytic approach.

· These links strengthen around the transition to secondary school.

孩子的动机并不是「天生就那样」，而是对 「我在这个学校有没有位置、我能不能学会」的实时回应。 校园里每一次真实的被看见，都可能在数据上留下痕迹。

Motivation isn’t fixed; it is a moment-to-moment response to feeling they belong and can succeed. Every authentic moment of being seen at school can leave a trace in the data.

父母如果少一点控制、多一点「我相信你可以自己做选择」， 青少年在面对同伴压力时，是否更有能力说「不」？

When parents offer more autonomy support and less control, are adolescents better able to say “no” to peer pressure?

想让孩子「不盲从」，秘诀不是更严厉的管控，而是 在安全边界内，把决定权一点点还给他们。 被相信过的孩子，更有底气对同伴说「这次我不想跟」。

Protecting teens from negative peer influence is less about tighter control and more about returning decision-making power within safe boundaries. Teens who are trusted at home stand stronger among peers.

中国 LGB 人群的社会支持、自尊与心理困扰
Sources of social support, self-esteem and psychological distress among Chinese lesbian, gay and bisexual people
研究方向 Focus

在中国文化与社会脉络下，不同来源的社会支持（家庭、朋友、伴侣、社群） 如何影响 LGB 人群的自尊与心理健康。

In Chinese contexts, how support from family, friends, partners, and community relates to self-esteem and psychological distress among LGB individuals.

方法 & 主要结果 Methods & key findings

· 问卷研究，分析不同支持来源的独特作用。
· 朋友与伴侣支持与更高自尊、更低心理困扰显著相关；家庭支持重要，但往往不足。
· 少数应对压力（如隐蔽、内化污名）与更高的心理困扰相关。

· Survey analyses tease apart unique roles of each support source.
· Friend and partner support strongly relate to higher self-esteem and less distress; family support matters but is often limited.
· Minority stress processes (e.g., concealment, internalised stigma) predict greater distress.

Take-home message

很多 LGB 个体，是靠「自己选来的家人」——朋友与伴侣撑过来。 对实务工作者来说，创造安全的社群与团体空间，和改变家庭一样重要。

Many LGB individuals survive through “chosen family” – friends and partners. For practitioners, building safe communities and groups is as vital as working with families.

害怕慈悲 · 情绪调节 · 情绪性进食 Fears of compassion · emotion regulation · emotional eating
害怕慈悲、情绪调节困难与情绪性进食的调节中介模型
The relationship between fears of compassion, emotion regulation difficulties, and emotional eating in college students: A moderated mediation model
研究方向 Focus

当一个人害怕被安慰、害怕对自己好时，会不会更难调节情绪，从而更容易用「吃」来麻痹自己？

Explores whether fearing compassion makes it harder to regulate emotions, leading to more emotional eating as a coping strategy.

方法 & 主要结果 Methods & key findings

· 大学生样本，采用调节中介模型分析。
· 害怕慈悲与情绪性进食之间的关联，很大程度上通过情绪调节困难实现。
· 某些个体差异（如自我相关特质）会改变这种路径的强度。

· College student sample with moderated mediation modelling.
· The link between fears of compassion and emotional eating is largely mediated by emotion regulation difficulties.
· Individual differences (e.g., self-related traits) shape the strength of this pathway.

Take-home message

很多所谓的「意志力不够」，其实是 在没有安全情感资源时，只好用食物来托住自己。 真正有帮助的介入，是同时练习情绪调节和与温柔建立更安全的关系。

What looks like “weak willpower” is often an attempt to hold oneself together without safe emotional resources. Effective interventions build both regulation skills and a safer relationship with kindness.

害怕慈悲量表在中国文化情境下的验证（共同第一作者）
Validation of the fears of compassion scale in a Chinese cultural context (co-first author)
研究方向 Focus

将「害怕慈悲」这一概念，系统地引入中文语境，检验其结构与测量性质， 为后续研究与临床实践打下基础。

Introduces and validates the construct of fears of compassion in Chinese samples, providing a solid measurement base for research and practice.

方法 & 主要结果 Methods & key findings

· 量表翻译与反向翻译，采用探索与验证性因素分析。
· 结果支持量表的结构效度与信度；害怕慈悲与自我批评、更高的心理困扰等指标显著相关。
· 为后续探讨「害怕温柔」如何影响心理健康提供了工具。

· Translation/back-translation with exploratory and confirmatory factor analyses.
· Supports the scale’s reliability and structural validity; fears of compassion correlate with self-criticism and distress.
· Provides a key tool to study how fearing kindness affects mental health.

Take-home message

在华人语境中，害怕温柔、害怕被看见脆弱是普遍而重要的主题。 有了可用的量表，研究与临床都能更精细地看见这部分经验。

In Chinese contexts, fear of softness and fear of showing vulnerability are central themes. A validated measure allows us to see and work with this more precisely.
