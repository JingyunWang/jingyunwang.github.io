发展心理学博士（荷兰乌特勒支大学）
应用心理学学士 · 心理健康教育硕士
具备 11 年心理实践经验，接受过多取向心理咨询与团体带领训练。 PhD in Developmental Psychology (Utrecht University, the Netherlands).
BA in Applied Psychology · MA in Mental Health Education.
11+ years of psychological practice including multi-modal counseling and group work training.

研究与实践兴趣 Research & practice interests

  • 青少年与成年早期的自主性发展、亲密关系与家庭动力。 Autonomy development, close relationships, and family dynamics in adolescence and emerging adulthood.
  • 被霸凌与同伴关系中的创伤经验与复原力。 Peer victimization, trauma in peer relations, and resilience.
  • 高敏感、高责任感群体的情感需求与职业/学术倦怠。 Emotional needs and burnout among highly sensitive, high-responsibility groups.
  • 东西方文化语境下，“好孩子/好人”脚本的内化与松动。 How “good child / good person” scripts are internalized and softened in East-West cultural contexts.

已开展的部分活动 Previous spaces & activities

  • 多场关于密集纵向数据（ILD）、发展心理学与自我发展主题的工作坊与学术分享。 Workshops and talks on intensive longitudinal data, developmental psychology, and self-development.
  • 心理读书会、深度分享小组、长期同伴支持团体的设计与带领。 Book circles, deep sharing groups, and long-term peer support circles.
  • 为本科与硕士学生提供研究指导与人生/职业发展陪伴。 Research supervision and life/career accompaniment for bachelor and master students.

我在这座岛上的位置 My place on this isle

我不把自己放在“专家”或“导师”的高位，而是一个
有工具、有路径，也愿意承认自己仍在学习 的同行者。

我的承诺是：
· 用扎实的理论与严谨的研究素养，守护对你的负责。
· 用充足的自我觉察与伦理边界，守护对关系的尊重。
· 用柔软但清晰的眼睛，看见你，也提醒你看见自己。 I don’t see myself as “expert on a pedestal”, but as a fellow traveler
with tools and paths — and with my own ongoing learning.

My commitments:
· Grounded theory and rigorous research to stay accountable to you.
· Self-awareness and ethical boundaries to stay respectful to the relationship.
· Eyes that are soft yet clear: to see you, and to remind you to see yourself.

未来的愿景 Vision going forward

我希望，寻屿可以慢慢发展成：
· 一个兼具深度与温度的心理空间，
· 一个连接学术、实践与普通人生活的桥，
· 一个让敏感的人、思考太多的人、走得太久太累的人，
  可以暂时靠岸的地方。 My hope is for Yunisle to become:
· A psychological space with both depth and warmth,
· A bridge between academia, practice, and everyday life,
· A shore for those who are sensitive, who think “too much”, who have walked too far alone.
