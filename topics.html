咨询与团体空间 · 我们会在哪些形式里相遇？ Spaces we meet in · formats of the journey

这些年，我一直在不同空间里陪伴来访者和学员：
心理测评、个体咨询、团体咨询、团体沙盘、心理教育工作坊、心理学读书会…… 它们像是不同形状的容器，承载的是同样的主题：复原力、情绪与需求、自主性与关系、自我探索与接纳、人际互动与共情沟通。

Over the years I’ve worked across several formats – assessment, individual counselling, group therapy, group sandplay, psychoeducational workshops, and psychology reading circles . They are different containers for similar themes: resilience, emotions and needs, autonomy and relationships, self-acceptance, interpersonal patterns, and empathy.

For recurring struggles in relationships. A group becomes a “living mirror” where you can safely try new ways of relating – expressing, saying no, and staying present.

常见话题 · 生命里几个关键节点 Key topics · crossroads in a life

下列话题，是我在个体咨询、团体、工作坊与读书会中最常遇见， 也最愿意陪伴的几个方向。你可以把它们当作「入口」， 选一个此刻对你最有共鸣的起点。

These are the themes I most frequently meet – and care deeply about – in individual work, groups, workshops, and reading circles. Treat them as doorways and choose the one that fits where you are now.

适合：青少年 / 大学生 / 研究生 / 职场人 For: teens · students · early career

当「好好读书/工作」不再足够：学习与工作动力耗尽

When “work hard” stops working: motivation burnout

明明很努力，却越来越提不起劲；一边自责「是不是我太懒」，一边觉得怎么做都不对。 我们会把你的动力放回家庭、学校/职场与自我期待的系统里重新理解， 而不是只用「意志力」给自己施压。

You’ve tried your best yet feel increasingly drained. Part of you says “I’m lazy”; another part knows it’s not that simple. We place your motivation back into the systems of family, school/work, and self-expectations.

  • 中考/高考/升学或转专业、转行前后的动机起伏。
  • 「我到底是为谁在拼？」——为父母、为简历，还是为自己？
  • Motivation dips around transitions (exams, changing majors or jobs).
  • “Who am I doing this for?” – parents, CV, or a future self.
适合：青少年 / 青年 / 为人父母者 For: adolescents · young adults · parents

「不想再这么懂事了」：亲子与原生家庭

“I’m tired of being the good child”: parents & family of origin

一边不想让家人失望，一边又想为自己活一点；既舍不得，也想拉开一点距离。 我们会在中国文化与代际期待的脉络下， 重新理解「孝顺」「独立」与界限，而不是简单地「断联」或「继续忍」。

You don’t want to disappoint your family, yet you long for more room to be yourself. We revisit “filial piety”, “independence”, and boundaries within Chinese cultural and intergenerational contexts.

  • 「听话孩子」长大后，如何学会说“不”。
  • 父母如何从控制转向支持，让关系里有更多呼吸空间。
  • Learning to say “no” after a childhood of being “the good kid”.
  • Helping parents shift from control to support.
适合：青年 / 伴侣 / 正在关系中摇摆的人 For: young adults · partners · those in-between

亲密关系与界限：在「靠近」与「不失去自己」之间

Intimacy & boundaries: staying close without losing yourself

总是在关系里用力讨好、控制，或者逃跑？我们会从 依恋、自主性与过往关系经验 出发，看你是如何学会「这样爱人和被爱」的， 并在团体与工作坊中练习新的互动方式。

If you find yourself pleasing, controlling, or fleeing in relationships, we explore how your ways of loving and being loved emerged from attachment, autonomy, and past relationship histories, and practise new patterns in groups and workshops.

  • 在爱里保有自主，而不是把自己全部交出去。
  • 从「我有问题」转向「我们的互动可以怎么不一样」。
  • Keeping autonomy in love without shutting down connection.
  • Moving from “I am the problem” to “how can we meet differently?”.
适合：留学生 / 海外华人 / 跨文化伴侣 For: international students · migrants · cross-cultural couples

在两种（或更多）文化之间生活

Living between cultures and countries

在一个地方读书、工作、恋爱，心却还留在另一个地方；既想留下，又挂念回去。 我们会把移居、学业、职业与关系选择放在同一条时间线上， 看清你真正的优先级，而不是只根据「别人觉得好的路」做决定。

You study, work, and love in one country while part of your heart lives elsewhere. We map your migration, study, work, and relationship choices on a shared timeline to see your real priorities.

  • 如何在「安全」与「自由」之间做出不后悔的选择。
  • 在跨文化关系中，同时照顾两个人的背景与需求。
  • Choosing between safety and freedom without abandoning either.
  • Honouring both partners’ cultures in a relationship.
适合：在人生节点停下脚步的人 For: anyone pausing at a life crossroads

自我探索与生命意义：给「不知道想要什么」一点时间

Self-exploration & meaning: staying with “I don’t know yet”

不少来访者会说：「我已经完成了很多任务，但好像还没真正开始自己的生活」。 我们会用发展心理学与存在主义的视角， 帮你看清此刻所处的阶段与可能性，在迷茫中整理出一条属于自己的方向感。

Many people tell me: “I’ve ticked many boxes, but haven’t really begun my own life.” We use developmental and existential perspectives to locate where you are and what could come next.

  • 从「我应该成为什么样的人」走向「我愿意成为什么样的人」。
  • 把迷茫视为过渡期，而不是失败证据。
  • Shifting from “Who should I be?” to “Who am I willing to become?”
  • Framing confusion as transition, not failure.
适合：对 AI / 数字心理有兴趣的个体与团队 For: individuals & teams working with AI

AI 与心理健康：在效率时代保留「人味」

AI & mental health: keeping the human in the loop

当我们把心理支持带进 App、机器人或线上课程，怎样才能在追求效率的同时， 不丢失自主、关系与意义这三个核心？ 这也是我在咨询与研究之外，持续探索的边界。

When we move support into apps and AI tools, how do we protect autonomy, connection, and meaning rather than just efficiency? This is a boundary I keep exploring alongside counselling and research.

  • 为 AI 心理工具做概念设计与伦理讨论。
  • 协助团队把发展心理学与自我决定理论，落在产品与干预流程中。
  • Concept and ethics work for AI-based mental health tools.
  • Embedding developmental and SDT insights into products and interventions.
我们可以如何设计一段「旅程」 How we can shape a journey together

不同阶段、不同议题，需要不一样的节奏。
下面是常用的几种旅程形式，你可以把它们看作打样—— 具体会根据你的时间、状态与资源微调。

Different questions call for different rhythms. The formats below are templates we can adapt to your time, energy, and resources.

总的来说，一段旅程大致会经历：

In essence, most journeys move through:

1 次 × 75 分钟：先把最急的部分说清楚
1 × 75 min · Clarifying what hurts most now

适合：在某个节点上卡住，需要一个安全空间理一理思路、做一个暂时决定的人。

For when you’re stuck at a crossroads and need a safe place to think and make a provisional choice.

FORMAT · 短程旅程 FORMAT · Short journey
3–5 次：理解一个模式，找到一个新选项
3–5 sessions · Understanding one pattern

适合：围绕一个核心议题（如学习动力、亲子冲突、自我怀疑），想要既有理解，也有具体行动方案。

For one focused theme (e.g. motivation, conflict with parents, self-doubt) where you want both insight and concrete steps.

FORMAT · 深度旅程 FORMAT · Deep journey
8–12 次：重写一段生命叙事
8–12+ sessions · Rewriting a life story

适合：长期困扰（如完美主义、自我价值感低、复杂家庭背景），需要在时间中慢慢调整关系与自我位置。

For long-standing issues (perfectionism, low self-worth, complex family histories) that need time to untangle and reweave.

FORMAT · 团体 / 工作坊 FORMAT · Groups / workshops
主题式旅程：一起走的一小段路
Themed journeys · walking together

适合：想在安全结构中与他人同行的人，例如：学习动机小组、亲子共学、跨文化适应、 情绪与需求、人际互动、共情沟通，或 AI 与心理健康对话等。

For those who want to walk with others: e.g. motivation groups, parent–teen programmes, cross-cultural circles, emotion & needs work, relational workshops, or AI & mental health dialogues.

这只是「起始模板」。真正的旅程，会根据你的节奏共同调整：
有时是一小段冲刺，有时是慢慢同行；有时在你生活平稳期提前铺路，有时在风浪中暂时托一托你。

These are starting points. The real journey is tailored together:
sometimes a short sprint, sometimes slow companionship; sometimes preparing in calm periods, sometimes holding you during storms.

已经走过的几条旅程 · 你可能会在其中认出自己 Journeys I’ve walked with others · you might recognise yourself here

STUDENT & YOUNG ADULT PATHS STUDENT & YOUNG ADULT PATHS
学习、职业与「我到底要成为什么样的人？」
Study, career, and “Who do I want to become?”

来访者通常从一句话开始：「我不确定要不要继续这样下去」——读这个专业、留在这个国家、或待在这个团队。 我们会一边看现实条件，一边看内在需要与价值，而不是只问「哪个更体面」。

Many journeys begin with: “I’m not sure I want to keep doing this” – this major, this PhD, this job, or this country. We consider both practical constraints and inner needs/values, not only “what looks better”.

  • 从「我必须成功」到「我可以定义什么是对我来说的成功」。
  • 在转专业/转行/出国/回国的选择里，看清安全、自由、家庭与自我的权重。
  • 把「拖延」「学不会」翻译成：系统给了你怎样的任务和压力。
  • Moving from “I must be successful” to “I define success for myself now”.
  • Weighing safety, freedom, family, and self in choices about changing majors, jobs, or countries.
  • Translating “I’m lazy” into a clearer picture of demands and supports in your environment.
RELATIONSHIP & FAMILY PATHS RELATIONSHIP & FAMILY PATHS
在关系里，既不消失，也不孤立
Staying present in relationships without disappearing

很多旅程都从「我是不是太敏感/太冷漠/太自私」开始，最后落在： 「原来在那样的环境里，我已经尽力了。接下来我想换一种方式对自己和别人负责。」

Many journeys start with “Am I too sensitive / cold / selfish?” and end with: “I did my best in that environment. Now I want to relate differently to myself and others.”

  • 陪伴父母与青少年一起，从「成绩至上」走向「关系与成长并重」。
  • 帮助伴侣看见：争吵背后，其实是各自的恐惧与渴望，而不是谁对谁错。
  • 协助长期背负家庭责任的人，慢慢学习「把自己也算进家庭的一员」。
  • Supporting parents and teens to move from “grades first” to “relationship and growth together”.
  • Helping couples see the fear and longing beneath conflict, instead of just right or wrong.
  • Working with those who have carried family roles alone to see themselves as part of the family, not just its backbone.
如果你想和我一起，开启一段旅程 If you’d like to start a journey together

We decide on format: single session, short/longer journey, or a group/sandplay/workshop format, aligned with your constraints.

Mid-way review & adjustment

Closing & after-effects

We devote time to close intentionally, naming what you’ve gained, so the impact can continue in your everyday life.

如果你现在还没准备好开始，只是想先看看「我适不适合来谈」，也完全可以写信来告诉我—— 有时，把犹豫本身说出来，就是很好的第一步。

If you’re not ready to start but want to ask whether this might fit you, you’re welcome to write just about that. Naming hesitation is already a meaningful first step.
