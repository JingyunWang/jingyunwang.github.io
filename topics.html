话题与旅程 · 从真实困惑出发的陪伴 Topics & journeys · starting from real questions

心理咨询师 · 个体/团体 Counsellor · individual / group 团体/工作坊带领者 Group & workshop facilitator 人际关系工作坊主创 Relational workshop designer

在这里，你不会看到「虚空的大词」，只有贴着生活的议题：
韧性与心理健康、动机、情绪与需求、自主性与关系、自我探索与接纳、人际互动模式、共情沟通…… 有时也会延伸到跨文化迁徙、职业选择，以及科技（如 AI）如何改变我们的生活与心。

Here you won’t find vague slogans, but real-life themes: resilience and mental health, motivation, emotions and needs, autonomy and relationships, self-exploration and acceptance, interpersonal patterns, and empathic communication – sometimes extending into cross-cultural life, career choices, and how technology (including AI) shapes our inner world.

这些旅程，可能发生在 个体/团体咨询、潜意识探索（沙盘/绘画/OH 卡）、心理教育工作坊、心理学读书会、研究与工具共创里—— 有时是一句被认真听见的话，有时是一段静心冥想的呼吸。

These journeys may unfold in individual and group sessions, unconscious exploration (sandplay, drawing, OH-cards), workshops, reading circles, or co-created research tools – sometimes through one sentence that lands, sometimes through a quiet mindfulness practice.

咨询与团体空间 · 我们会在哪些形式里相遇？ Spaces we meet in · formats of the journey

这些年，我一直在不同空间里陪伴来访者和学员：
个体/团体咨询、潜意识探索（OH 卡/绘画/沙盘等）、心理学读书会、心理教育工作坊、开发研究与干预工具…… 它们像是不同形状的容器，有的偏向深度体验，有的偏向知识与实践整合，有的偏向长期共创。

Over the years I’ve supported people through individual and group counselling, unconscious exploration (OH-cards, drawing, sandplay), reading circles, psychoeducational workshops, and the development of research and intervention tools . Each is a different container – some for deep experience, some for learning and practice, some for long-term co-creation.

常见话题 · 围绕几个核心痛点 Key topics · where it often hurts

下面每一块，都是我最常陪伴，也最熟悉的主题：
韧性与心理健康、动机、情绪与需求、自主性与关系、自我探索与接纳、人际互动模式与共情沟通。 你可以把它们看成一个个「我现在最在意的地方」。

These are the themes I work with most: resilience and mental health, motivation, emotions and needs, autonomy and relationships, self-exploration and acceptance, interpersonal patterns and empathy . Treat them as “what matters most to me now”.

适合：青少年 / 大学生 / 研究生 / 职场人 For: teens · students · early career

总是很累却停不下来：动机与倦怠

Always tired, never off-duty: motivation & burnout

外界看起来你很「上进」，内心却越来越空。休息会愧疚，继续又透不过气。 我们会一起梳理真正的想要、长期的压力源和可调整的部分， 让动力从「被逼着」慢慢回到「愿意」。

From the outside you look driven; inside you feel flat. Rest brings guilt, pushing on brings exhaustion. We disentangle what you truly want, chronic pressures, and what can shift, moving from “forced” motivation toward willing engagement.

  • 学习/科研/工作长期高压后的「我再也撑不动了」。
  • 在重要转折（考研、升学、换工作、是否继续读博）前重新整理方向。
  • Burnout after years of study, research, or work pressure.
  • Re-orienting around key transitions (exams, job changes, PhD decisions).
适合：青年 / 为人子女与为人父母者 For: adult children & parents

一直很懂事，却很难为自己活：亲子与家庭期待

Always the “good child”: parents & expectations

你习惯照顾每个人的感受，却不太会问「我呢？」。 我们会放在中国文化、代际故事与家庭角色里， 一起练习：既不撕裂关系，也不给自己的人生缺席。

You’ve learned to care for everyone’s feelings except your own. Within Chinese culture, family stories and roles, we practise staying connected without disappearing.

  • 在「孝顺」和「为自己做选择」之间找到新的平衡。
  • 从长期的情感照顾者，慢慢学会也被照顾。
  • Finding a new balance between filial duty and personal choice.
  • Shifting from only caring for others to allowing care for yourself.
适合：恋爱、婚姻或亲密关系中反复挣扎的人 For: those struggling in close relationships

靠近就怕被吞没，拉远又觉得孤单：亲密与边界

Afraid to be too close, scared to be alone: intimacy & boundaries

有时你很想靠近，有时又突然退得很远；要么过度付出，要么完全关机。 我们会从依恋、自主性与旧有互动模式出发， 练习用更稳定、真实的方式去爱与被爱。

At times you cling, at times you shut down. We explore attachment, autonomy, and old patterns, practising steadier, more authentic ways of relating.

  • 从「要么黏在一起，要么完全拉开」走向可谈、可商量的亲密。
  • 在关系中保有自我，而不是用牺牲证明价值。
  • Moving from all-or-nothing to negotiated closeness.
  • Keeping a sense of self in love instead of proving worth by sacrifice.
适合：留学生 / 海外华人 / 计划移居的人 For: international students & migrants

在两种生活之间摇摆：留学、移居与回不回去

Torn between lives: study, migration & “Should I stay?”

一边想要更多自由，一边害怕不稳定；一边向往远方，一边牵挂家人。 我们会结合发展阶段、职业路径与家庭期待， 帮你做出更清醒、少一点后悔的选择。

You long for freedom but fear instability; you dream of elsewhere yet miss home. We look at developmental stage, career paths, and family, supporting choices you can stand by.

  • 在安全感与探索欲之间找到自己的比例。
  • 把签证、移民、职业规划放回整个人生时间线上看。
  • Finding your own ratio of safety and exploration.
  • Placing visas, migration, and career plans on your life timeline.
适合：在关键节点想「停下来看看自己」的人 For: anyone pausing to re-meet themselves

完成了很多任务，却还没开始做自己：自我探索与接纳

Ticked all the boxes, still not yourself: self-exploration & acceptance

你可能已经按部就班地完成了「标准答案」，却总有种淡淡的空心感。 我们会用发展心理学与存在主义视角， 看清你所处阶段、内在价值与未被活出来的部分。

You’ve done everything “right” yet feel strangely hollow. With developmental and existential lenses we locate where you are and what parts of you haven’t been lived.

  • 从「我应该成为什么样的人」走向「我愿意长成怎样的自己」。
  • 通过写作、绘画、正念练习等方式，慢慢与自己和解。
  • Shifting from “Who should I be?” to “Who am I willing to become?”.
  • Using writing, drawing, and mindfulness to make peace with yourself.
适合：不会说、不敢说、说了总是吵的人 For: those who can’t speak, or only fight

不会表达，只会爆发：情绪、需求与共情沟通

Can’t express, only explode: emotions, needs & empathy

你可能习惯「要么忍，要么炸」，要么全部吞咽，要么一句话把关系弄僵。 我们会一起练习辨认底层情绪与未说出口的需求， 用更不伤己也不伤人的方式沟通。

You either endure until you explode, or hold everything in. We practise naming core emotions and unmet needs, and speaking in ways that protect both you and the relationship.

  • 学会区分情绪背后的需要，而不只是「控制情绪」。
  • 在团体/工作坊中练习具体对话句式与倾听方式。
  • Seeing needs behind feelings instead of just “controlling emotions”.
  • Practising concrete phrases and listening skills in groups or workshops.
我们可以如何设计一段「旅程」 How we can shape a journey together

不同议题，需要不同的深度与节奏。有人适合单次聚焦， 有人需要多次、短程或长程陪伴； 有人只想一对一，有人则在团体里更容易松下来。 下面是常见的几种组合方式。

Different questions call for different depth and pace: some people benefit from a single focus session, others from short- or longer-term work; some prefer 1:1, others relax more in groups. These are common combinations.

大致来说，一段旅程会经过：

Most journeys move through:

1 × 75–90 min · Clarifying what feels most stuck

适合：在某个选择上摇摆（要不要继续、要不要离开），想先理清思路， 或者想体验一次咨询再决定是否继续的人。

For crossroads decisions (continue or leave, stay or move) or when you’d like to experience one session before committing further.

4–6 sessions · One theme, concrete next steps

适合：围绕动机、情绪与需求、自主性与关系等核心议题， 想要既有理解，也有练习和行动计划的人。

For focused work on motivation, emotions and needs, autonomy and relationships, combining insight with practice and action plans.

FORMAT · 长程一对一 / 深度探索 FORMAT · Longer 1:1 / depth work
8–20+ 次 · 重写长期模式与身份故事
8–20+ sessions · Reworking long-term patterns

适合：多年累积的主题，如完美主义、低自我价值感、复杂家庭/创伤经历、 深层孤独感等。会穿插潜意识探索、正念、关系体验。

For long-standing issues such as perfectionism, low self-worth, complex family or trauma histories, and deep loneliness. May include unconscious work, mindfulness, and relational experiments.

FORMAT · 团体 / 工作坊系列 FORMAT · Group / workshop series
4–8 次 · 多人同行的一段路
4–8 meetings · Walking with others

适合：想在结构清晰又有人陪伴的环境里，一起探索 学习动机、亲子与人际关系、情绪与需求、共情沟通、正念与自我照顾等主题。

For those who prefer a clear structure and companionship while exploring motivation, family and relationships, emotions and needs, empathic communication, mindfulness, and self-care .

以上只是「打样」。真正的旅程，会根据你的时间、经济情况、身心状态来微调：
可以先从一次开始，也可以直接规划短程或长程；可以只做一对一，也可以在合适的时候加入团体。

These are templates. The real journey is tailored to your time, resources, and current capacity:
you can start with a single session, plan a short or longer series, stay 1:1 or later add a group when it feels right.

已经走过的几条旅程 · 你可能会在其中认出自己 Journeys I’ve walked with others · you might recognise yourself here

为保护隐私，这里只用「路线图」的方式呈现， 把许多人的旅程，凝练成几个可以参考的轨迹。

To protect confidentiality, these are not individual stories but route sketches distilled from many journeys.

STUDENT & YOUNG ADULT PATHS STUDENT & YOUNG ADULT PATHS
从「必须优秀」到「允许自己是人」
From “I must excel” to “I’m allowed to be human”

很多学生和青年会从一句「我好像快撑不住了」开始。 在几次会谈里，我们一起对齐现实条件、内在需要与价值， 把「我不行」翻译成「原来在这样的系统里，我已经很用力」， 再慢慢讨论：接下来，我要为谁、以怎样的节奏继续走。

Many students and young adults begin with “I don’t know how much longer I can keep this up.” We align real constraints with inner needs and values, translating “I’m failing” into “I’ve done my best in this system,” then exploring who and what you want to keep moving for.

  • 在动机与倦怠之间重新找到「刚刚好的努力」。
  • 把升学、求职、出国/回国放回整个人生图景，而不是孤立的 KPI。
  • 学会把「我很累」说出口，而不是默默扛到崩溃。
  • Finding a sustainable level of striving between drive and burnout.
  • Placing study, work, and migration choices in the bigger life picture.
  • Learning to say “I’m exhausted” before reaching breaking point.
RELATIONSHIP & FAMILY PATHS RELATIONSHIP & FAMILY PATHS
在关系里，既不消失，也不被淹没
Staying present in relationships without drowning

很多旅程从「是不是我太敏感/太自私」开始，到最后变成： 「在那样的童年和环境里，我是这样学会爱人和保护自己的。现在我想尝试一种新的方式。」

Many journeys start with “Am I too sensitive / selfish?” and end with “Given my childhood and context, this is how I learned to love and protect myself. Now I’d like to try a different way.”

  • 陪伴父母与青少年，从「成绩优先」转向「关系与成长并重」。
  • 帮助伴侣看见：争吵背后是各自的恐惧与渴望，而不是单纯对错。
  • 协助长期扮演照顾者的人，慢慢练习把自己的需要，也放进关系里。
  • Supporting parents and teens to move from “grades first” to “relationship and growth together”.
  • Helping couples see fear and longing beneath conflict, not just right versus wrong.
  • Working with long-time caregivers to include their own needs in relationships.
如果你想和我一起，开启一段旅程 If you’d like to start a journey together

你不需要「想好所有问题」才来。 真正重要的，是先为自己开辟一小块稳定的空间。
无论是单次还是多次、短程还是长程、一人还是一群人， 我们都会先确认：目前对你最现实、也最温和的方式是什么。

You don’t need everything figured out before reaching out. What matters first is a small, steady space for yourself. Whether it’s one session or many, short- or longer-term, individual or group, we’ll begin by finding what is realistic and kind for you now.

STEP 01 STEP 01
简短来信 / 留言
A short email / message

用几句话写下你现在最在意的事、所在国家/时区， 以及你大致的想法：更偏向单次对话， 还是可以考虑多次咨询 / 团体。

A few lines on what you’re facing now, where you are (country/time zone), and whether you’re leaning toward a single session or open to a series / group.

STEP 02 STEP 02
初次会谈 · 对焦与评估
First meeting · clarifying & assessing

我们会一起确认目前最重要的议题，并评估安全性和适配度： 是否适合由我来陪伴，或需要其他本地/医疗资源。

We clarify your main concerns and assess safety and fit: whether I’m the right person to support you, or whether local / medical services are needed.

STEP 03 STEP 03
选择节奏：单次、多次、短程、长程
Choosing rhythm: single, short, or longer

根据议题与资源，一起商量是单次澄清， 还是4–6 次短程， 或8–20+ 次长程深度旅程， 并确定是一对一还是团体/工作坊更合适。

Depending on your situation, we decide between a single clarity session, a 4–6 session short journey, or an 8–20+ session deeper process, and whether 1:1 or group formats fit best.

STEP 04 STEP 04
中程回顾与微调
Mid-way review & adjustment

在旅程中途，我们会一起检视： 哪些对你有帮助，哪些需要调整——包括频率、形式（个体 ↔ 团体）、以及是否加入正念练习或潜意识探索。

Mid-way we review what helps and what doesn’t – including frequency, format (individual ↔ group), and whether to bring in more mindfulness or unconscious work.

STEP 05 STEP 05
结束与余波 · 把收获带回日常
Closing & after-effects · taking it into life

在结束前，我们会用一整次会谈好好收尾： 总结你获得的理解、工具和关系经验， 讨论后续如何自我照顾、何时可能再次需要支持。

Before ending we dedicate a full session to closure: naming insights, tools, and relational experiences, and planning self-care and when you might reach out again if needed.

如果你现在还没准备好开始，只是想先问问「我适不适合来谈」， 也完全可以写信告诉我—— 有时，把犹豫本身说出来，就是很好的第一步。

If you’re not ready to begin but want to ask whether this could be right for you, you’re welcome to write just about that. Naming your hesitation is already a meaningful first step.
