About

I am a developmental psychologist and counsellor working with young people, families, and those who care for them. My work weaves together autonomy, relationships, and mental health, often using intensive longitudinal designs to trace the small steps of change.

Underneath the methods is a simple commitment: when people feel seen and held with honesty and warmth, something true begins to move.

Therapy & Care

In therapy and guidance, I offer a grounded, gentle space for:

  • Questions of identity, autonomy and “who am I becoming?”
  • Family, romantic and peer relationship patterns
  • Shame, self-worth and the fear of not being “enough”
  • Stress, sensitivity and emotional overwhelm

My approach integrates evidence-based methods with deep listening, clear boundaries and compassion.

Research & Projects

My research focuses on autonomy development, close relationships and youth mental health, with a special interest in intensive longitudinal data.

  • Autonomy in the context of family, peers and school
  • Rumination, co-rumination and victimisation
  • Within-person processes captured by daily diaries and EMA
