HOME · INVITATION

The Yunisle

The inner isle of loving, becoming, and being whole.

I am Jingyun, inviting you to live freely, feel deeply, and step into your inner world.

This is a space for people who have spent years being “who they were expected to be” and now feel a quiet pull toward something truer. You might be standing at the edge of change — between countries, careers, identities, relationships, or chapters of life.

What if your doubt, exhaustion, and longing are not problems to fix, but doorways to a different way of living?

On the Yunisle, we walk a journey from experience → awareness → action → self-creation. With psychology as method and philosophy as backbone, we rebuild how you relate to yourself, others, and time.

Here, every soul finds its isle; every step carries a story, echoing forward and shaping the self and journey.

This is where you meet who you truly are — and where our most authentic selves encounter one another, unfold, and co-create meaning together.

You are welcome here if you are:

  • In transition — moving countries, careers, or roles
  • Questioning old expectations and inherited scripts
  • Longing for deeper relationships, with self and others
  • Curious about how science and inner life can meet
THEORY & METHOD

Inner Isles · Theory & Method

Self-determination · exploration · insight · action · gentle, grounded change.
Self-Determination Theory Autonomy & Connection Developmental Psychology Phenomenology

The Yunisle is rooted in Self-Determination Theory, which sees human beings as wired for autonomy, competence, and relatedness. When these needs are supported, we grow; when they are blocked, we twist ourselves into shapes that no longer feel like home.

From a developmental lens, I pay attention to how your history — family, school, culture, and relationships — has shaped your ways of coping, attaching, and striving.

From a philosophical lens, I draw on existential thought: the question is not “Who should I be?” but “Who am I, if I dare to live as myself?”

Three movements guide our work together:

  • Exploration — noticing experience with curiosity rather than judgment
  • Insight — making sense of patterns, needs, and meanings
  • Action — taking small, aligned steps in your real life

Voices that resonate here:

  • “When we are no longer able to change a situation, we are challenged to change ourselves.” — Viktor Frankl
  • “The curious paradox is that when I accept myself just as I am, then I can change.” — Carl Rogers
  • “Freedom is what we do with what is done to us.” — Jean-Paul Sartre
MEET YUN

Meet Yun

Developmental psychologist · counsellor · companion on inner journeys.

I am Jingyun Wang — Yun — a developmental psychologist trained in China and the Netherlands, with over a decade of experience in counselling, psycho-educational work, and group facilitation.

My research weaves autonomy, relationships, and youth mental health, often through intensive longitudinal designs. My practice weaves evidence-based psychology with a deeply human, compassionate presence.

I care about people who feel “between worlds”: between cultures, languages, careers, identities, or expectations. My work is not to fix you, but to walk beside you as you remember your own voice.

Some things I bring to our work:

  • Training across multiple therapeutic approaches
  • Experience with youth, families, and cross-cultural journeys
  • Research on autonomy, peer relations, and mental health
  • Love for both data and stories, structure and mystery

Vision:

To help people move from being “the expected one” — the good child, good student, good worker — toward becoming a self-aware, self-creating presence, in honest relationship with self and others.
