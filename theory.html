理念与方法

从「知道很多」到「真的活出来」。

寻屿不是再给你一套新的「应该」，而是用扎实的心理学理论， 帮你把已经知道的东西，慢慢变成真的能活在关系、选择和日常里的力量。

理念关键词：自我决定 · 依恋与关系 · 发展视角 · 探索—领悟—行动。

自我决定理论：在「我愿意」里，长出「我可以」。

自我决定理论（Self-Determination Theory）认为，人要长期健康地发展，需要三种重要的心理需要： 自主性、胜任感、连结感。

  • 自主性：我可以为自己做选择，而不只是被推着走。
  • 胜任感：我有机会尝试、练习，并感到「我在变得更好」。
  • 连结感：我在关系中被看见、被在意，而不是孤立无援。

在寻屿，我们会从你真实的情境出发，一起看：
你是如何在不知不觉中牺牲了自主？又是如何在「过度懂事」中失去了连结？
然后一步步长出「我可以为自己负责」的力量。

不同流派的对话：让许多声音一起陪你。

在方法上，我会整合多种心理学传统，以「对话」而不是「谁对谁错」的方式使用它们：

精神分析 · 无意识与关系模式

「我们重复不能记得的东西。」—— 弗洛伊德（Sigmund Freud）

这里我们关注你在关系中反复遇到的情绪和情节，它们从哪里来，又指向哪里。

人本主义 · 成为自己

「当我愿意做自己时，人们才开始改变。」—— 罗杰斯（Carl Rogers）

这里我们练习在安全的关系里，不再压抑需求，而是慢慢允许真实出现。

存在主义 · 面对有限的人生

「生命的意义不是被发现的，而是被承担的。」—— 维克多·弗兰克尔（Viktor Frankl）

这里我们谈自由、责任、孤独、时间和死亡——那些你大概早就感到，只是没人和你认真说过的话题。

认知与行为 · 把领悟落在生活里

「不是事情本身困扰我们，而是我们对事情的看法。」—— 埃利斯（Albert Ellis）

这里我们一起设计具体、可执行的小实验，把新的理解慢慢编织进日常。

寻屿的三步：探索 · 领悟 · 行动

无论是个体对话、工作坊还是长期项目，寻屿都大致遵循这三步：

  1. 探索（Explore）：用故事、图像、量表、写作等方式，把你此刻的生命经验铺开来看。
  2. 领悟（Insight）：在理论与对话中，看见模式、理解自己，找到情绪背后的逻辑和需要。
  3. 行动（Act）：不追求「一次改变全部」，而是设计小而稳的行动，持续校准方向。

你不是在「修理自己」，而是在与自己重新相遇。
寻屿只是那座中途的小岛，让你有机会补一口气、看一看远方，再继续出发。

Theory & Practice

From “knowing a lot” to actually living differently.

Yunisle is not here to give you another list of “shoulds”. It weaves solid psychological theories into the fabric of real life, so that what you already know can slowly become something you can live and embody.

Core threads: self-determination · attachment & relationships · developmental lens · explore–insight–action.

Self-Determination Theory: from “I have to” to “I choose to”.

Self-Determination Theory suggests that sustainable well-being grows when three needs are met: autonomy, competence, and relatedness.

  • Autonomy: feeling that your choices are truly yours, not just reactions to pressure.
  • Competence: having chances to try, practice, and feel “I am getting better at this”.
  • Relatedness: feeling seen and cared for, not left alone with everything.

On Yunisle, we explore how you may have quietly given up autonomy, or over-performed connection, and how you can rebuild a sense of “I can stand with myself”.

Dialogues between traditions: many voices by your side.

Methodologically, I integrate multiple psychological traditions, not to decide who is “right”, but to let them speak with each other around your life.

Psychoanalytic · Unconscious patterns

“We repeat what we cannot remember.” — Sigmund Freud

We look at recurring emotional scenes and ask: where did this begin, and what is it trying to protect?

Humanistic · Becoming oneself

“The curious paradox is that when I accept myself just as I am, then I can change.” — Carl Rogers

We practice allowing your needs and feelings to exist without immediate judgment.

Existential · Facing a finite life

“Life is never made unbearable by circumstances, but only by lack of meaning.” — Viktor Frankl

We talk honestly about freedom, responsibility, time, and what makes a life worth carrying.

Cognitive & Behavioral · Bringing insight into daily life

“People are disturbed not by things, but by the view they take of them.” — Albert Ellis

We design small, concrete experiments that gently redirect old patterns.

The three movements of Yunisle: Explore · Insight · Act

Across sessions, workshops, or longer-term projects, we tend to follow three movements:

  1. Explore: lay out your experiences through stories, images, questionnaires, or writing.
  2. Insight: name the patterns, make sense of your feelings, and connect them to your needs.
  3. Act: choose small, realistic steps that honor what you’ve discovered.

You are not “fixing” yourself. You are meeting yourself again.
Yunisle is simply a small island along the way, where you can rest, reorient, and then continue.
