理论与路径 · Theory & Pathways

不是为了“给你做一套模型”，而是帮你看清：我此刻是如何与自己、他人与命运相处的。

Theory & Pathways

Not a model to fit you into, but a lens to see how you relate to yourself, others, and what life brings.

一，自我决定理论：从“被推动”到“自己选择”

自我决定理论（Self-Determination Theory, SDT）认为，人要真正感到有生命力， 需要三种核心心理需要：自主感、胜任感、连结感。

  • 自主（Autonomy）：我的选择，是否出自内心，而非仅仅出于压力与讨好？
  • 胜任（Competence）：我是否在做“我能逐渐掌握”的事，而不是永远感到无力？
  • 连结（Relatedness）：在重要关系里，我是否被看见、被尊重，而不是被条件化地爱？

在寻屿，我们不会只问“你要做什么决定”， 而会一起追问：“这个决定，是谁在替你做？恐惧？习惯？还是逐渐清晰的自己？”

1. Self-Determination Theory: from “pushed” to “chosen”

Self-Determination Theory (SDT) suggests that to feel truly alive, we need three core psychological needs: autonomy, competence, and relatedness.

  • Autonomy: Are my choices coming from within, or mainly from pressure and pleasing?
  • Competence: Am I doing things I can gradually grow into, instead of feeling endlessly helpless?
  • Relatedness: In important relationships, am I seen and respected, not loved only on conditions?

On Yunisle, we don’t just ask “What will you decide?” but also: “Who is deciding for you — fear, habit, or a self that’s becoming clearer?”

二，心理学家的对话：多种声音，同一个问题

我喜欢把不同流派的心理学家，请到同一张“想象中的圆桌”来对话：

  • 罗杰斯（人本主义）：“当一个人能成为他自己时，他才真正开始成长。”
  • 弗洛伊德（精神分析）：“未被意识到的，终将以命运的形式重演。”
  • 维克多·弗兰克尔（存在主义）：“在任何境遇中，人依然有选择态度的自由。”
  • 阿德勒（个体心理学）：“你的人生难题，多半是‘如何与他人共处’的问题。”

在寻屿，并不是选一个“唯一正确的流派”，而是把这些声音都请来， 看它们如何照亮你生命中的某一块角落。

2. A round table of psychologists: many voices, one human question

I like to imagine different schools of psychology sitting at the same table:

  • Rogers (humanistic): “When a person is able to be their true self, growth begins.”
  • Freud (psychoanalytic): “What is not brought to consciousness returns as fate.”
  • Viktor Frankl (existential): “In every situation, we retain the freedom to choose our attitude.”
  • Adler (individual psychology): “Most life problems are, at heart, problems of how we live with others.”

On Yunisle, we don’t worship one “correct” school. We invite these voices to speak to different corners of your life, together.

三，从探索到行动：探索—领悟—行动

不是停在“想明白”，而是走进“活出来”。

3. From exploring to living it: explore–understand–act

We’re not here only to “figure it out”, but to live differently.

探索 · Exploration

用温和而诚实的方式，整理你的经验：童年记忆、关系模式、文化与时代的影响。 我们会用提问、书写、对话与日常观察，慢慢看清那些一直存在、但你还没有名字的东西。

领悟 · Insight

这里的“领悟”，不是一句“我懂了”，而是： 当你再次走进同样的情景，身体与心，能有一点点不一样的反应。

行动 · Embodied Action

行动不是“逼自己做更多”，而是练习：
· 在说“好”之前，先问问自己“我真的愿意吗？”
· 在关系里多表达一次真实感受；
· 在某个时刻，不再只听恐惧的声音。

Exploration

We gently and honestly map your experiences: early memories, relationship patterns, cultural and generational stories. Through questions, writing, dialogue, and small observations, we name what has long been there but remained unseen.

Insight

Insight here is not “I get it now” as an idea, but: when you walk into a familiar situation again, your heart and body react just a little differently.

Action

Action is not forcing yourself to “do more”, but practising: saying “yes” a bit more honestly, naming a feeling in a relationship, or not letting fear be the only voice steering the wheel.

这套路径，如何落实在具体活动中？

· 在研究中，它体现在：密集纵向数据、日记研究、发展轨迹的捕捉；
· 在团体与工作坊中，它体现在：安全边界、彼此见证、小步行动；
· 在个人陪伴中，它体现在：慢慢练习把“知道”变成“能做”。

如果你想看到更具体的项目形式，可以去看看 「共创与项目」 页面。

How does this show up in real projects?

· In research: intensive longitudinal data, diary designs, tracing developmental patterns;
· In groups & workshops: clear boundaries, mutual witnessing, tiny experiments in daily life;
· In individual journeys: slowly turning “I know” into “I can live this”.
